History of Odisha

Odisha's Heritage: A Journey Through Art, Architecture, and Tradition

The history of Odisha dates back to ancient times, with evidence of early settlements in the region. The Kalinga region, which corresponds to modern-day Odisha, was known for its early civilization and trade connections.

The Kalinga region was a significant power in ancient India. The Kalinga War, fought in 261 BCE between the Mauryan Emperor Ashoka and the Kalinga Kingdom, is a key event in Odisha's history. The war led to a major transformation in Ashoka, who adopted Buddhism and promoted non-violence.

After the Kalinga War, Odisha was under Mauryan control and later came under the rule of various local dynasties, including the Satavahanas.

The Ganga dynasty (not to be confused with the later Ganga dynasty in southern India) ruled Odisha from the 11th to the 15th century. The period is noted for its architectural achievements, including the construction of the famous Jagannath Temple in Puri.
