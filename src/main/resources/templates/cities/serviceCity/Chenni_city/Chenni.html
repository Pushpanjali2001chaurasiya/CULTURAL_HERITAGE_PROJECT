Chennai: Where Ancient Traditions Meet Contemporary Life

The region around Chennai has been inhabited since ancient times. Archaeological evidence suggests that the area was a significant center of trade and culture, with connections to ancient Tamil kingdoms.
The area was part of the Chola dynasty's realm during the medieval period, which contributed to its early cultural and economic development.
In the early 16th century, the Portuguese established a presence in the region, focusing on trade and missionary work.The Dutch also set up trading posts along the Coromandel Coast during this period.
The modern history of Chennai began in 1639 when the British East India Company acquired a small strip of land from the local Nayak rulers to establish a fortified settlement. This area was known as Fort St. George and became the foundation of what is now Chennai.
