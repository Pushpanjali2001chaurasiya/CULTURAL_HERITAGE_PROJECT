The recorded history of Kolkata began in 1690 when the English East India Company arrived in Bengal to consolidate its trade business.

Kolkata's history dates back to ancient times when the area was part of the Bengal region. The original inhabitants were indigenous tribes. The name "Kolkata" is derived from the Bengali word "Kolikata," which is believed to have its roots in the term "Kilkila," a reference to the sound of the local bird, the crow.
British East India Company: The modern history of Kolkata begins in the 17th century when the British East India Company acquired the three villages of Kalikata, Sutanuti, and Gobindapur from the local rulers in 1698. The Company established Fort William and made Calcutta its headquarters, gradually transforming it into a significant trading post.
Post-Independence Era: After India's independence, Kolkata continued to be a major cultural, commercial, and educational center. The city's economy, while facing challenges, remains vibrant with its diverse industries and rich heritage.
