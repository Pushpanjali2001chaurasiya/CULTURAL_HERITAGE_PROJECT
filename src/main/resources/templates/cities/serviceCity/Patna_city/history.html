History of Patna

History, Best Places to Visit and Travel Guide of Patna – One of Seven Sacred Cities of Hinduism, Bihar, India

Patna, the capital city of Bihar, India, boasts a rich historical legacy that spans over 2,500 years. Originally known as Pataliputra, the city was a prominent center of power and culture in ancient India. Founded by Ajatashatru in the 5th century BCE, it rose to prominence as the capital of the Magadh Empire. Under the Mauryan Empire, particularly during the reign of Emperor Ashoka, Patna became a major hub of political and economic activity. The city flourished as a cultural and intellectual center, attracting scholars and monks, including the famous Chinese traveler Xuanzang.

During the Gupta period, Patna continued to thrive as a center of learning and art. However, the city faced decline with the rise of the Muslim dynasties in the 12th century but regained prominence under the Mughal Empire. It served as a significant administrative and trade center during this period. The British East India Company also recognized Patna’s strategic importance, and it became a key site during the colonial era.

Today, Patna stands as a vibrant city that reflects its ancient grandeur through its historical monuments, educational institutions, and cultural heritage. It remains a testament to the enduring legacy of one of India’s most historically significant cities.
