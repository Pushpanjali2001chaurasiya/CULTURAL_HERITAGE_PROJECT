Swaminarayan Akshardham, Delhi

Swaminarayan Akshardham in Delhi is a stunning Hindu temple and spiritual-cultural complex that stands as a testament to India’s rich heritage. Inaugurated on November 6, 2005, by Pramukh Swami Maharaj, the temple is dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan and reflects the essence of Indian art, culture, and spirituality.

The main monument, or mandir, is an architectural marvel made of pink sandstone and white marble, intricately carved with depictions of deities, dancers, musicians, and flora and fauna. The central figure in the temple is a 11-foot high statue of Bhagwan Swaminarayan, surrounded by other deities. The mandir’s design follows traditional Vastu Shastra and Pancharatra Shastra principles, ensuring that every element is in harmony with nature and spirituality.

A relatively new attraction, this massive temple complex was built by the BAPS Swaminarayan Sanstha spiritual organization and opened in 2005. It's dedicated to showcasing Indian culture. As well as the astonishing architecture of the pink stone and white marble shrine, the complex includes sprawling garden, sculptures, and boat ride. Allow plenty of time to explore it thoroughly -- at least half a day. Do note that cell phones and cameras are not permitted inside.

Location: National Highway 24, near Noida Mor, New Delhi.
Entry Cost: Free. However, tickets are required to view the exhibitions. Opening Hours: 9.30 a.m. until 6.30 p.m. (last entry). Closed Mondays.
