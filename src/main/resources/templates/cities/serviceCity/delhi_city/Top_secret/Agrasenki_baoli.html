Agrasen Ki Baoli , Delhi

Agrasen Ki Baoli is a historical stepwell located on Hailey Road, near Connaught Place in New Delhi. This architectural marvel is 60 meters long and 15 meters wide, with 108 steps leading down to the well.

Believed to have been originally built by the legendary king Agrasen, the current structure likely dates back to the 14th century, during the Tughlaq or Lodi period1. The baoli is protected by the Archaeological Survey of India under the Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act of 1958.

Location: Old Delhi, near the Red Fort and Jama Masjid.
