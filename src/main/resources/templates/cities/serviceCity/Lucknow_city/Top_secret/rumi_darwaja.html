Rumi Darwaza, Lucknow

The Rumi Darwaza, also known as the Turkish Gate, is a magnificent gateway located in the heart of Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh. Constructed in 1784 by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula, it stands as a remarkable example of Awadhi architecture.

The Rumi Darwaza is an imposing structure that features a large central arch topped with a half-spherical dome. The intricate floral carvings and the chhatri (umbrella) at the top add to its grandeur.

Historically, the Rumi Darwaza was constructed as part of a “Food for Work” program initiated by Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula during a severe famine. This initiative provided employment to the people of Lucknow, allowing them to earn food in exchange for their labor.

The Rumi Darwaza holds significant cultural and historical importance. It has become a symbol of Lucknow’s rich heritage and is often included in heritage tours of the city.
