Dilkusha Kothi, Lucknow

Dilkusha Kothi in Lucknow is a fascinating historical site that offers a glimpse into the architectural and cultural heritage of the region.Built around 1800 by Major Gore Ouseley, a British resident and friend of Nawab Saadat Ali Khan, the Kothi was initially intended as a hunting lodge for the Nawabs of Oudh1. Over time, it also served as a summer resort, providing a retreat from the bustling city life

The architecture of Dilkusha Kothi is a striking example of the English Baroque style, which was quite unique for its time and location1. The design of the Kothi is said to bear a resemblance to Seaton Delaval Hall in Northumberland, England, which was designed by Sir John Vanbrugh1. Unlike traditional Indian architecture, Dilkusha Kothi did not have an inner courtyard, making it stand out with its taller structure and patterned walls.
