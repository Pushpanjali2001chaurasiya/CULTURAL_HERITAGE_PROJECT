Indra Gandhi Planetarium, Lucknow

The Indira Gandhi Planetarium, located in the Surajkund Park neighborhood of Lucknow, is a remarkable institution dedicated to the exploration and education of astronomy and space science. Named after the former Prime Minister of India, Indira Gandhi, the planetarium was inaugurated in 2003 and has since become a significant landmark in the city.

One of the most striking features of the Indira Gandhi Planetarium is its unique architectural design. The building is shaped like the planet Saturn, complete with its iconic rings. This design choice not only makes the planetarium visually appealing but also serves as an educational tool, providing visitors with a tangible representation of one of the most recognizable celestial bodies in our solar system. The structure is surrounded by a water pool with fountains, adding to its aesthetic appeal and creating a serene environment for visitors.
