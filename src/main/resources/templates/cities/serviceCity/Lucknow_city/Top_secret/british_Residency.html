British Residency, Lucknow

The British Residency in Lucknow is a significant historical site with a rich past. Built between 1780 and 1800, it served as the residence for the British Resident General at the court of the Nawab of Oudh. The Residency is most famous for its role during the Indian Rebellion of 1857, where it was besieged for 87 days2. The ruins of the Residency still bear the marks of cannon shots and are preserved as they were at the end of the siege

During this period, it became the epicenter of the Siege of Lucknow, where British forces and their allies were besieged by Indian rebels for 87 days1.
