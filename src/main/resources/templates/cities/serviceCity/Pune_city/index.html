PUNE THINGS TO DO

The recorded history of Kolkata began in 1690 when the English East India Company arrived in Bengal to consolidate its trade business.

Pune, often regarded as the cultural capital of Maharashtra, has a rich history deeply intertwined with its cultural heritage. The city's history is marked by its prominence as a center of education, political influence, and artistic expression.
Pune rose to prominence during the 17th century as a major base for the Maratha Empire under the leadership of Shivaji Maharaj. The city became an important political and military center during the reign of the Maratha Empire and later the Peshwas, the prime ministers of the empire.
Pune boasts a variety of historical architectural styles. The Shaniwarwada Fort, built in 1732, is an iconic symbol of Maratha heritage. The Lal Mahal, originally a royal residence for Shivaji Maharaj, and the Aga Khan Palace, which played a significant role in the Indian freedom struggle, are also notable landmarks.
