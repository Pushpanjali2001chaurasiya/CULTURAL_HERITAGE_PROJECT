Haji Ali Dargah, Mumbai

The Haji Ali Dargah is one of Mumbai’s most iconic landmarks, situated on an islet off the coast of Worli in the Arabian Sea. This revered site is both a mosque and a dargah (tomb) dedicated to Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari, a wealthy merchant-turned-Sufi saint from Uzbekistan1. Built in 1431, the dargah is a stunning example of Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring a white marble structure that stands out against the blue sea

The dargah is accessible via a narrow causeway that connects it to the mainland, which can only be traversed during low tide2. This pathway adds to the mystical allure of the site, making the journey to the dargah a unique experience. The structure itself comprises a central shrine, a mosque, and a sanatorium. The shrine’s marble courtyard is adorned with intricate mirror work and kaleidoscopic patterns, reflecting the artistic heritage of the Mughal era

Haji Ali Dargah is not just a religious site but also a cultural hub that attracts people of all faiths. Every Thursday and Friday, thousands of devotees visit the dargah to seek blessings and fulfill their vows2. The serene atmosphere, coupled with the rhythmic sounds of the waves, creates a tranquil environment for prayer and reflection. The dargah also hosts various cultural and religious events, making it a vibrant part of Mumbai’s social fabric
